AFM Microscope

Atomic Force Microscope

Atomic Force Microscope (AFM) is a powerful tool used for imaging and characterizing surfaces at the nanoscale. It works by using a tiny probe with a sharp tip that scans the surface of a sample. As the probe moves across the surface, it interacts with the atoms or molecules, creating a three dimentional map of the surface topography. The AFM can provide high-resolution images with details down to the atomic level. It is widely used in various fields of research, including nanotechnology, materials science, biology, and physics, to study surface properties, measure forces, and manipulate individial atoms or molecules.

Cypher ES Environmental AFM

Cypher ES Environmental AFM is a type of atomic force microscope designed for imaging and analyzing samples in various environmental conditions, such as liquids or gases. It allows researchers to study the surface properties of materials at a nanoscale level. It's a powerful tool used in scientific research and nanotechnology.
